🧑🏻‍💼 What I Do & How I Think

My role is to grow and scale our internal engineering capability - building both the strategy and the environment that enables our people to deliver great software, while also defining how we effectively partner with vendors, and independant 3rd-parties, to complement and extend our delivery model.

Right now, I’m contributing to the evolution of our engineering capability at Specsavers by shaping and supporting a long-term strategy for how we build and scale our software delivery. That includes influencing direction in areas such as:

  • Practice (craft, community, consistency)
  • Value stream engineering (optimising how we deliver end-to-end)
  • Core engineering (standards, tools, environments)
  • Innovation (time, space and sponsorship to explore ideas)
  • Governance and metrics (because we can’t improve what we can’t see)

I care deeply about platform thinking, developer enablement, and how teams can balance autonomy with alignment. I try to zoom between strategic and tactical levels - one minute I’m talking about engineering operating models, the next I’m in the weeds of a CI/CD design. I’m happiest at the intersection of strategy and delivery - connecting high-level goals to the day-to-day work of our engineers. I move between coaching, problem-solving, facilitating alignment, and telling stories that bring our technical vision to life.

🧭 My Leadership Philosophy

I believe in creating clarity, removing friction, and enabling people to do their best work.

My leadership style is rooted in servant leadership and is outcome-oriented - I prioritise enabling and supporting my teams to thrive. That said, I’m not hands-off: I take clear ownership of outcomes, set direction with intention, and step in confidently when leadership and decision-making are needed. I don’t believe in micromanagement - I trust people to own their outcomes. My role is to remove blockers, ask useful questions, and help us stay aligned to our mission and values.

🛠 How I Make Decisions

I use a mix of data, context, and instinct. I tend to ask:

  • Is this scalable and sustainable?
  • Does this align with our direction and values?
  • Who will this affect, and how can we include them?

I prefer consensus where possible, but I will make clear decisions when needed. I’m happy to take accountability and explain the rationale behind any decision I make.

💼 My Working Style

  • I’m motivated by the why. I ask a lot of questions to make sure we’re solving the right problem.
  • I want the context, not just the conclusion. Share the thinking behind decisions - especially if you’re asking for a steer.
  • I prefer clarity over perfection - I’d rather see an early idea than a polished doc that’s too late to change.
  • I prefer the problem over the solution - even if you’ve already picked a path, walk me through the decision.
  • I like structured thinking but messy whiteboards - don’t be afraid to show half-formed ideas.
  • I communicate best via diagrams, metaphors, and real-world parallels.
  • I favour asynchronous tools and clear, complete communication. I’ll try to give you everything you need in one message.
  • I default to optimism and forward momentum, but I value dissent and devil’s advocacy.

⚖️ Strength of Feeling Scale

I sometimes use a Strength of Feeling Scale Scale (1-10) ⚖️ to signal how strongly I feel about something. A “2” means I’ll go with the flow. A “9” means I care a lot and might be harder to sway. If I add 4/10 to a message or converstion this is me telling you how strong my conviction is.
